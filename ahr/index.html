Why UK Companies Are Desperate
For Pension Transfers And
How To Leverage Their Incentives

With so much uncertainty in the markets, investors seeking safe havens for their money have been pouring funds into government-backed bonds. Government bonds are traditionally seen as low-risk investments - however, their return has been low for quite some time. And with Brexit, Covid-19, and the Bank of England holding interest rates, the expectation is that the yields will remain low for quite some time.

As a result, Cash Equivalent Transfer Values (CETV) for Final Salary Pension Schemes have hit new highs.

What Is A Cash Equivalent Transfer Value (CETV)
And How You Can Benefit From It?

Simply put, CETV is the amount your pension fund will offer you if you decide to transfer your benefit pension into a Self Invested Personal Pension (SIPP) contribution scheme.

When calculating a CETV, the most important investment return factor used by a scheme actuary is the risk-free investment return based on UK gilt yields - which are currently at a record low level.

This means that by transferring your pension, you can get significantly higher returns, and you can also get a sum of money right away.

Why UK Companies Are Willing To Offer Huge
Incentives For Pension Transfers

Final Salary Schemes represent a vast black hole on the balance sheets of countless businesses. Employers are willing to take huge hits in terms of initial costs to offload their pension commitments. Pension transfers take away any long term liability and risk presented to the future of their company.

This is why companies are actively incentivising members with generous enhancements when calculating a transfer value for you to take your Company Final Salary Pension to a Self Invested Personal Pension (SIPP).
